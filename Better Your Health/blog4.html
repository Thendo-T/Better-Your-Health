Dining out

Health 01 Feb. 2017

Think about the last time you went out with your friends. Did you make choices that were in line with your healthy-eating habits? If you said no, don't beat yourself up. 

It can be hard to make good choices particularly in terms of food. Sometimes family and friends can unintentionally make changing your eating habits difficult. Even everyday activities — such as going out to eat with friends, or having dinner with family — may seem difficult.

These strategies can help keep you in the right mindset at your next social gathering.

Before you head out the door, try these ideas:

• Have a small snack before you leave so you won't be starving when you arrive at the party/restaurant.

• Check out the menu of the restaurant online/ call in to ask for healthier food options before you leave, so you give yourself the opportunity to choose healthy options.

• Stand away from the buffet table so you won't be tempted to continuously graze.

• Socialize more than you eat.

• Consume extra calories only as a special treat.

• Work out or take a walk before you go out (to burn some calories).

• Decide how many drinks you will have, before you go out.

Please note! Alcohol provides empty calories without any nutrients, so the calories can add up quickly depending on the kind of drink and number of glasses drank.Alcohol can also make you feel hungrier, leading you to eat more. When you drink, consider mixing alcohol with nonalcoholic drinks, such as  diet soda or tonic water, to save on calories.

Remember : Change is a process, not an event
