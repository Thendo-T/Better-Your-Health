Emotional eating

Health 01 Feb. 2017

There's no denying it: food can make you feel good. If you've noticed you resort to eating food during sad, happy, or stressful times, you may be practicing emotional eating.

What is emotional eating?

Emotional eating means that you eat for reasons other than hunger, defined as recurring, unconscious attraction toward foods that fulfill an emotional void or distracts you from what's really bothering you. This may include emotions ranging from stress, sadness, frustration, and anxiety to name a few.

Emotional eating can also be linked to happy times," Think about how you celebrate big achievements and special occasions, or even just define fun outings. We treat ourselves to our favorite foods to define a moment of pride or joy.

Emotional eating can be okay in moderation. It's when this behavior becomes a bad habit where it can be an issue both physically and emotionally it can lead to:

•weight gain

• high blood pressure

• type 2 diabetes

• high cholesterol

Tips to stop/curb emotional eating: 

1. Identify any triggers you have. Some common examples include:boredom, a fight with a friend or spouse /poor performance reviewFind self-care replacements for emotional        eating triggers:read a book,call a friend,walk around the block,take a shower or bath .

2. Find alternatives to rewarding yourself with something other than food something like painting your nails, or taking some 'me time' to read or journal." Going for massage or hiking with friends.

3. Find other ways to deal with stress. Hobbies like jogging, dancing or exercise regularly.

4. Slow down when eating. By slowing down and savoring every bite, you’ll not only enjoy your food more, but you’ll also be less likely to overeat.

5. Remove temptations “out of sight,out of mind”. Identify what foods you typically gravitate towards when eating emotionally and try removing them from your eyesight.

6. Ask yourself if you're really hungry.

7. Get enough rest and sleep. Feeling tired can cause you to turn to food to improve your energy levels.

8. Get support. Talk to someone you can confide in or a therapist.
